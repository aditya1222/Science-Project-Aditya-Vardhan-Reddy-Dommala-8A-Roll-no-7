How Masks are made:

  • Surgical face masks are produced from non-woven fabric, which has greater filtration of bacteria, virus and air permeability than woven material.
  • The material most widely used to make them is polypropylene though other materials likepolystyrene, polycarbonate, polyethylene or polyester can also be used
  • The middle layer in a medical mask is made with a nonwoven melt blown process featuring electrostatic charging. The two outer layers are made with the nonwoven spun bond process. The 3 nonwovens layers are then calendared and together form the filter media.

Things That happen when they get disposed in bins:

  • Used masks being disposed in the bins has created an unsanitary and unsafe environment .
  • People who handle the waste also have a high chance of catching the virus while handling the possibly contaminated waste.
  • The masks in bins can sometimes overflow and cause some of them to fly away to other places by wind and spread all its contaminants along the way

How it contributes to pollution:

  • Single-use masks are typically made from polypropylene, a fossil fuel-derived plastic that can take hundreds of years to break down.
  • They also shed tiny harmful microplastics into our waterways(Rivers,oceans,etc), which are then consumed by unsuspecting fish

Ways and Remedies to take care of this new normal:

  • Proper Disposal techniques for different kinds of used masks:
  • Cloth mask (Wash) – Should be washed properly and frequently and left to hang and air dry.
  • N95 respirator – When removing the mask, hold the edge of the straps attached to take of the mask. Don’t touch the inside part of the mask. Wash hands before and after it.
  • Surgical mask (Fold, tie, wrap) – It should be removed chin upwards and please take care to remove it from the strings and be careful not to touch the front portion while taking off the mask.
